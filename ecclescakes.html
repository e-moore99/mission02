Eccles Cakes

This recipe is for the Eccles Cakes my parents made and sold in their cafes when I was growing up, it was always a hit!

Ingredients:

  • 1 packet of feather flake or puff pastry.
  • 400g dried currants
  • 35g butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1.5 tsp cinnamon
  • White sugar to top
  • Milk, for milk wash

Method:

  1. The night before; melt the butter, and mix the butter, brown sugar, and cinnamon together into a caramel. Add to the currants and mix. Cover and allow to marinate.
  2. Preheat the oven to 180Â°C. Defrost the pastry.
  3. Cut the pastry into squares. Place a few tablespoons of filling and pinch the pastry closed.
  4. Turn the eccles cake over so the closing is underneath it, and squish it down into a patty shape.
  5. Repeat this process until you have used all of the filling.
  6. Place the eccles cakes on a baking tray, allowing space for them to puff.
  7. Using a sharp knife, score the top making 3 parallel lines. Brush the tops with milk and sprinkle with white sugar.
  8. Bake until golden brown, 15-20 mins. Eccles cakes are best enjoyed warm.

Notes:

  • I have found this ratio uses an entire packet of pastry so if you halve it you will have pastry leftover.
  • I tend to use a coarser sugar on top like demerara as I think the texture is nicer, but it doesn't really matter what you use.
